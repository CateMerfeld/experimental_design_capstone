Is incarceration an effective means of controlling crime?¶

Is the effect on different types of crimes the same?¶

Description of the data:¶

  • Crime & incarceration by state from 2001-2016
  • Columns added for:
    - crimes per capita
    - percent of the population incarcerated

  • Excludes jail populations

Methods:¶

  • Group data by annual change in incarceration rate

Group A - increased incarceration rate

Group B - decreased incarceration rate

  • Examine differences in median crime rates between groups
  • Group and analyze data by type of crime

Hypotheses:¶

H0 : There is no significant difference between the median crime rate for records in Group A and Group B.

Ha : There is a significant difference between the median crime rate for records in Group A and Group B.

Results:¶

No significant difference in total crime rates between the two groups

Discussion:¶

Findings:¶

  • Group A did not have a significantly larger decrease in crime rates than Group B
  • No significant difference in crime rate change between groups for property crime and total crime

Limitations & next steps:¶

  • Determining which states are 'tough on crime'
  • Difficult to see clear trends over time

Take aways:¶

  • Growing body of evidence suggesting that incarcarceration does not reduce crime.
  • A 5% reduction in incarceration could lead to annual savings of up to $400,000,000
  • Reducing incarceration by 10% could save $8,000,000,000 annually
